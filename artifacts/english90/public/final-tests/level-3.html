Reading passage — B1 level academic text

The relationship between language learning and cognitive development has been the subject of considerable research in recent decades. Studies have demonstrated that individuals who learn a second language develop stronger problem-solving abilities, greater mental flexibility, and improved memory function compared to monolingual speakers. Furthermore, the evidence suggests that the process of switching between two languages exercises the brain in ways that may delay the onset of cognitive decline in later life. Despite these well-documented benefits, many students find language learning difficult to sustain over long periods. This is partly due to a lack of structured resources and partly because learners often underestimate the time and consistency required. Consequently, researchers argue that the most successful language learners are those who combine formal study with meaningful real-world practice — reading, speaking with native speakers, and engaging with authentic media. In conclusion, although language learning requires significant dedication, the cognitive and social benefits it provides make it one of the most valuable skills a person can develop.

Type the correct word or phrase. B1 grammar in context. / Зөв үг бичээрэй.

Question 44
When I arrived at the party, most of my friends _____ _____. (Past Perfect — they left before I arrived)
Correct answer: had already left (or "had left")
Question 45
She looked exhausted after the marathon. She _____ _____ _____ running for hours. (Past modal deduction)
Correct answer: must have been
Question 46
If she had studied more consistently, she _____ _____ _____ the B1 exam with distinction. (3rd Conditional)
Correct answer: would have passed (or "could have passed")
Question 47
The effect of bilingualism on the brain _____ _____ _____ extensively since the 1990s. (Present Perfect Passive)
Correct answer: has been studied (or "has been researched")
Question 48
I wish I _____ able to attend the graduation ceremony — I was sick that day. (Wish + past simple)
Correct answer: were (or "had been" — both accepted)
Question 49
_____ working full-time, she managed to study English for two hours every evening without fail. (Concession + noun/-ing)
Correct answer: despite (or "in spite of")
Question 50

Correct answer: will have completed (or "will have finished")
